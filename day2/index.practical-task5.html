Poem: The Golden Quiet

In the quiet dawn the gold awakes,
A ribbon of light on ancient lakes.
Hands that shaped it, stories they told —
Memory wrapped in metal, bright and bold.
Keep it close, but keep your heart free;
Gold may glimmer, but wisdom is the key.

— short reflective poem about gold and value

Lyrics: Song of the Goldsmith

Hammer sings on midnight's song,
Sparks that dance and dreams that long.

Every ring holds a tale to tell.
